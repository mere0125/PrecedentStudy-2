Part 2 — Layers of Analysis

What exists inside Surrogate?

Part 2 expands the datasheet into three layers of analysis: an ontological breakdown of the project’s components, a historical and contextual reading of the conditions surrounding its production, and a visual analysis of the representational strategies used to make the project legible.

02

Historical + Contextual Analysis

Surrogate emerges from overlapping histories of reproductive technology, health tracking, computational interfaces, and debates around bodily autonomy.

1980s–1990s

Commercial surrogacy, IVF, and assisted reproductive technologies become increasingly visible within medicine, law, and public culture.

2000s

Networked platforms and smartphones normalize continuous communication, location awareness, and remote coordination.

2010s

Wearables, pregnancy apps, Apple Health, and the Quantified Self movement turn bodily activity into data that can be tracked and interpreted.

Late 2010s

AI, algorithmic decision-making, and digital health systems expand the authority of software over intimate and medical decisions.

2022

Surrogate stages a speculative yet materially grounded question: what happens when care, pregnancy, and remote control become part of the same interface?

Technological Context

Mobile health interfaces, remote monitoring, data dashboards, biometric tracking, AI systems, and app-based management of daily life.

Social Context

Reproductive rights, bodily autonomy, commercial surrogacy, care labor, medical authority, privacy, and data capitalism.

Design Context

Critical design, speculative design, interactive documentary, performance art, HCI, and computational art as forms of social inquiry.

03

Visual + Aesthetic Analysis

The project communicates through multiple visual languages at once: medical documentation, interface design, performance, documentary media, and exhibition display.

Interface

The mobile app borrows the familiar language of health and productivity tools, making surveillance feel ordinary, usable, and intimate.

Performance

The artist’s body becomes both subject and medium, transforming a speculative proposal into an embodied social experiment.

Documentary

Video and interview formats blur fiction and reality, allowing the audience to follow the project as a process rather than a finished object.

Medical Archive

Clinical documents, legal procedures, and institutional language give the project credibility while also revealing the systems that regulate reproduction.

Installation

The work becomes spatial and public, shifting private reproductive decisions into a collective viewing and discussion environment.

Website

The web presentation functions as an archive, organizing the project through evidence, documentation, and navigable fragments.
